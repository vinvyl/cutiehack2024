Mars

Mars is the third closest planet to the Sun, with a distance of 146.15 million miles. Mars was named after the Roman god of war. It also has the name "Red Planet," because the iron minerals on the planet cause the surface to look red.
Mars is dry, rocky, and cold. It has a very thin atmosphere. It has seasons, extinct volcanoes, canyons, and weather. Mars is about half the size of Earth. It has two moons, named Phobos and Deimos.
